## ✅ What I Completed

### 1️⃣ Learned About IAM Policies

Studied identity-based permissions in AWS.

Understood:

- IAM policies are attached to users, groups and roles
- Permissions follow the identity
- Controls what actions identities can perform

Visualized:

```text
User / Role
      ↓
IAM Policy
      ↓
S3 Access
```

Learned:

- IAM policies define what an identity can do in AWS

---

### 2️⃣ Learned About Bucket Policies

Studied resource-based permissions for S3.

Understood:

- Bucket policies are attached directly to buckets
- Control who can access bucket resources
- Commonly used for cross-account access

Visualized:

```text
Bucket
   ↓
Bucket Policy
   ↓
Access Granted
```

Learned:

- Bucket policies control access at the resource level

---

### 3️⃣ Compared IAM Policies and Bucket Policies

Understood the differences between identity-based and resource-based permissions.

Visualized:

```text
IAM Policy
     ↓
Who Can Access

Bucket Policy
     ↓
Who May Access Bucket
```

Learned:

- IAM policies protect identities while bucket policies protect resources

---

### 4️⃣ Studied Access Control Lists (ACLs)

Learned how ACLs were originally used in S3.

Understood:

- ACLs can grant permissions to buckets and objects
- ACLs are considered legacy access controls
- AWS recommends using policies instead

Visualized:

```text
Object
   ↓
ACL
   ↓
Permission
```

Learned:

- ACLs exist mainly for backward compatibility

---

### 5️⃣ Studied AWS Permission Evaluation Logic

Learned how AWS evaluates permissions.

Understood:

- IAM policies are evaluated
- Bucket policies are evaluated
- Explicit deny overrides all allows

Visualized:

```text
IAM Policy
      +
Bucket Policy
      ↓
Final Decision
```

Learned:

- Explicit deny always takes precedence

---

### 6️⃣ Learned About Cross-Account Access

Studied how different AWS accounts access S3 resources.

Understood:

- IAM permissions alone are not enough
- Bucket policies must also allow access
- Both sides participate in authorization

Visualized:

```text
Account A
     ↓
IAM Policy

Account B Bucket
     ↓
Bucket Policy

     ↓
Access Granted
```

Learned:

- Cross-account access requires permissions on both sides

### 7️⃣ Studied S3 Block Public Access


- Block Public Access is a critical S3 security feature
